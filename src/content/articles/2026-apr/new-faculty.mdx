## New Faculty Interview!

By Suri Song (8Ti)

We are thrilled to welcome two talented new teachers to our school community in recent months, who will be teaching classes in the absence of Math SET, Ms. PV, and Chemistry and Biology SET, Ms. Bryant. Though they come from vastly different backgrounds, one from the rugged rugby pitches of Washington State and the forests of West Africa, the other from the graduate halls of Boston, both share a deep love for the magical moment when a student’s face lights up with understanding. Please join us in getting to know Ms. van Hoff and Ms. Phillips.

Originally from Redmond, Washington, Ms. van Hoff brings a truly global perspective to our science department. She earned her Zoology degree from Washington State University before teaching in Guinea's French education system, where she taught biology, chemistry, and physics. One of her most unforgettable experiences there was grabbing a biology class to watch a live cow dissection. She also earned her master’s degree at Trinity College in Ireland.

With seven years of teaching under her belt, Ms. van Hoff will be leading AP and Honors Chemistry, as well as 6th-grade Biology. Ms. van Hoff says that she is most excited to teach Stoichiometry and Gas Laws, along with “any chance to run a hands-on lab.” 
“One of my favorite things,” she says, “is explaining something and seeing a student get it. I enjoy the material I teach, and I love that students enjoy it, too.”
 
Outside the classroom, Ms. van Hoff has a wonderfully eclectic set of interests. She played collegiate rugby, loves a good oat milk chai latte, and spends her free time hiking, writing, reading, playing single-player RPGS (she highly recommends Red Dead Redemption 2 and Assassin’s Creed Odyssey), and doting on her dog. She is a devoted fan of The Lord of the Rings, her favorite movie being The Two Towers. Her favorite book is Terry Pratchett’s Good Omens. She also stated that she has a deep passion for horses. “If I had unlimited money,: she admits, “I would train horses.” Riding horses is one of her favorite hobbies. If you ever need an adventure story, ask her about the time she crashed a snowmobile. Her favorite moment of the day is waking up before her alarm.
 
Ms. Phillips joins us from having just completed graduate school at Boston University. While she originally planned to pursue a PhD, she soon realized her true calling was in the classroom. “I thought I was going to go into research,: she explains. “I went to Boston for a PhD, realized I didn’t like research, and decided I would rather teach. I really fell in love with teaching.”
 
This year, she will be teaching Prealgebra and Algebra 1, both subjects she finds deeply rewarding. “Everything about functions,” she says, is the most exciting thing to teach, especially direct and inverse variation, slope, lines, and data displays. She is particularly grateful for the flexibility at our school: “One of the nice things about BASIS Independent Bellevue is that there’s a lot of effort put into getting access to different curricula. It allows me to build and adapt materials, and learn a lot about the students.”
 
Ms. Phillips lives for the “Aha!” moment. “When you have students that put in the work to get to the point of understanding, there’s an immediate change when they reach that understanding.” Between undergrad and grad school, she took a meaningful break to work in homeless services. If she weren’t teaching, she would work in “something else that’s human-focused.” She notes, “I could see myself going back to social work someday.”
 
When she’s not teaching math, Ms. Phillips is picking gardening back up, diving into craft projects like needle felting, and losing herself in The Queen’s Thief book series. She treasures a good trip (she once had a wonderful journey through Canada's western provinces when she was younger and would love to repeat it as an adult) and finds joy in small moments: 

“When there’s sun in Seattle,” she laughs, “or a positive interaction with a stranger.” So far, she has been blown away by our school’s warmth. “Everyone’s been really welcoming. The amount of passion among the teaching staff has been really fantastic. It’s really nice to teach at a smaller school; the amount of care is incredible.”
 
Welcome, Ms. van Hoff and Ms. Phillips! We are so glad you’re here.
