Carrot Cake

## Cake Ingredients

- 2 1/2 cups of carrots
- 2 cups of  all-purpose flour
- 2 tsp of baking soda
- 1/2 tsp of kosher salt
- 2 tsp of cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp of nutmeg
- 1/8 tsp of clove
- 1 cup of vegetable or canola oil  
- 3/4 cup of light brown sugar, packed
- 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
- 3 large eggs
- 2 tsp of vanilla extract
- 3/4 cup of sour cream or full fat Greek yogurt 

## Cream Cheese Frosting Ingredients

- 1 cup of unsalted butter, softened at room temperature
- 16 oz of full fat cream cheese, softened to room temperature (use Philadelphia cream cheese for the best results)
- 4 1/2 cups of powdered sugar
- 1/2 tsp of vanilla extract

## Cake Instructions

1. Wash, peel, and grate your carrots; they should resemble very finely shredded cheese. Measure out 2 1/2 cups, not tightly packed, and set aside
2. Preheat the oven to 350F and grease 3 round 6” cake pans or 2 or 3 round 8” cake pans; line the bottom of each pan with a round sheet of parchment paper 
3. Whisk together the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and clove. Set aside
4. In a large bowl, whisk together the oil and sugars, then the eggs and vanilla
5. Then mix in the carrots and sour cream and pour in all of the dry ingredients and gently stir to combine
6. Evenly distribute the batter between the cake pans.
7. For 6″ pans, bake for 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick in the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Bake for 30-35 minutes if using 8″ pans

## Cream Cheese Frosting Instructions

1. Add the butter and cream cheese to the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment/hand mixer
2. Mix on medium to medium-high speed until completely smooth, then mix in the powdered sugar 1 cup at a time, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl after the 3rd cup
3. After adding all the sugar, scrape down the bowl again and mix in the vanilla extract until smooth
4. If the frosting is too thin, place the bowl in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes to thicken



While the cake chills, make your cream cheese frosting. If it’s too thin, chill the frosting for about an hour to thicken.
To assemble, level off the tops of each cake using a cake level or serrated knife. Then, spread a thin and even layer of frosting between each cake.
Spread a thick layer of frosting around the cake and then spread it evenly. Now slice and enjoy!
