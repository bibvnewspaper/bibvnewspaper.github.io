## Tips for aps

By Sam Liu (8Ti)

As spring approaches, high school students across the country are preparing for one of the most important tests of the year: the dreaded AP exams. These tests, taken each year in May, allow students to earn college credits as well as demonstrate their capabilities in the subjects. However, many students still struggle to find a good way to study that fits them. AP exams are scored on a scale from 1 to 5 with 1 being the lowest and 5 the highest score. Getting a good score on AP exams can help significantly in getting into a good college. Some study tips that can help include Starting Early. When you start studying a few weeks or maybe even months ahead of the exam, it can help your brain absorb all of the knowledge better, allowing you to memorize concepts better. Another great tip is to practice with past questions. Many of the past questions are similar to what will be expected on the exam, allowing you to get familiar to the formatting and hardness of the exam. Finally, you mustn’t study all day. Sleep, breaks, and relaxation are still key to doing well on the exams. A balanced study plan helps improve focus, memory, and overall performance for the exam.
