## Art Show 

By Molly Miao (8Ti)

 The Spring Art Show was hosted on the 15th this month, making it the first standalone art show hosted since the opening of the school. The event featured artwork from grades 3 through 10, along with activities like scavenger hunts. 
 Each grade had its own artistic prompt. Grades 3 and 4 selected their best works to be displayed. Grade 5 focused on radial symmetry, which used shapes and patterns around a central point. Grade 6 worked with value and form, using reference images and accurate proportions to create black and white portraits. Grade 7 drew in hyper-realism, using acrylic paint to create hyper-realistic paintings of foods. Grades 8, 9 and 10 used linear perspective for their pieces, with one point ranging to five point perspectives being used. 
 The gallery was decorated with paper flowers created by Mr. Brad’s and Ms. Dreskin’s classes. They were made of colorful tissue paper, and were used to create a spring theme. Visitors could participate in a scavenger hunt, which consisted of hunting for artworks with special features. For example, one could be asked to search for an artwork containing a butterfly. Completed scavenger hunt sheets could be entered into a raffle for a chance to win a prize. In addition, students who attended the event had the chance to send a post card to an artist, writing a note to them. 
 Food and drinks such as hot chocolate were also offered at the event.
