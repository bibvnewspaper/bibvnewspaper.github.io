## Predictions for the Oscars

By Rahika Agrawal

Marking the end of the award season is the upcoming 98th Academy Awards, also known as the Oscars. Here are my predictions for many of the popular categories, as well as opinions from our editor-in-chief, Suri Song (8Pt). For the Best Picture, having watched only half of the nominees, I can’t really provide a fair opinion; every movie I have watched from that list so far has been incredibly done. Our editor-in-chief thinks it should be One Battle After Another, and I certainly wouldn’t be complaining if that movie won. It was hilarious and overall amazing; kudos to Paul Thomas Anderson. On the subject of directors, our editor-in-chief also thinks that “Chloe Zhao deserves that best director Oscar, and if she doesn't get it, I'm blowing up The Academy… unless it’s Paul Thomas Anderson of course, his direction in OBAA was commendable”. Zhao did a really good job with Hamnet; it was a very well-paced and emotional movie. For Best Actor, it should either go to DiCaprio or Chalamet; both were incredible in One Battle After Another and Marty Supreme, respectively. Michael B. Jordan was fantastic in Sinners, but DiCaprio’s acting was just amazing in OBAA. Best Actress would have to go to Jessie Buckley for playing Agnes in Hamnet; her raw displays of emotion throughout made the movie for me. Either Sean Penn or Delroy Lindo has to get Best Supporting Actor for their roles as Steven Lockjaw in One Battle After Another and Delta Slim from Sinners, respectively. Teyana Taylor honestly portrayed her character so well in OBAA, so she needs to get Best Supporting Actress. For Best Animated Film, our editor-in-chief thinks that Zootopia 2 should win and I wholeheartedly agree; if KPOP Demon Hunters wins…nope. Best Film Editing would have to go to either F1 or Sinners; the graphics in F1 were incredible, and a really good job was done with Sinners; having Michael B. Jordan in the same scene next to himself takes crazy shooting and editing skills. F1 also better get Best Sound; the scoring and soundtrack were amazing. Either Avatar: Fire and Ash or F1 deserve Best Visual Effects; both were stunning. Wicked: For Good got 0 nominations, and our editor-in-chief, having watched both, thinks “Wicked: For Good was destined to be worse than the first part no matter what miracles the writers could pull. Act 2 of Wicked was where the problems originally lay in the musical, and I actually think the producers did whatever they could with the source material. It was an enjoyable movie, enhanced especially by its acting performances.” Yikes. On a happier note, Conan O’Brien is hosting again this year, which is his second year in a row!! Here’s a short remark from our editor-in-chief: “Oh, I love Conan. His commitment to the “bit” is admirable, and he’s simply hilarious. I loved him on Late Night, as well as the Oscars last year. The only thing I will ever applaud The Academy on is their host choice/comeback after the Jimmy Kimmel 2024 catastrophe.” Our editor-in-chief has deep, personal beef with Kimmel, apparently heighted after the 96th Oscars fiasco. “Conan’s probably one of my favorite talk show hosts, and I’m excited for his Oscars performance.”
