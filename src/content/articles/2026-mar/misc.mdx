## St. Patrick's Day

By Stephanie Song (8Pt)

Before March 17th was associated with green rivers and green attire, there was a religious man who heavily impacted Ireland’s culture and religion. Despite popular belief, St. Patrick wasn’t actually born in Ireland, but was brought there after being kidnapped as a teenager and essentially became a slave. During that period of slavery, he worked as a shepherd and had a religious awakening. He later escaped back to Britain, where he was born, but had a calling to return to Ireland to convert the Irish people. So, he returned as a Christian missionary and converted thousands of pagan Irish to Christianity. Because of the profound impact St. Patrick had on Ireland, after he died, he became part of Irish identity and heritage. In the Middle Ages, St. Patrick’s death day, March 17th, became a religious feast day to honor his contributions to Irish Christianity. The holiday exists worldwide today to celebrate the culture of the Irish diaspora living around the world.

## The Color Run

By Rebecca (7)

Three weeks ago, the school participated in the annual color run in celebration of Holi, the Indian festival of vibrant colors. The event was a joyful explosion of color as the participants ran the  2 miles around the campus. Balloons were decked out, and as runners ran around the school, colored powder was thrown at them. Other activities were also included like henna art and drumming. Traditional Indian treats were also included. This was full of excitement and cultural celebration, leaving participants with lasting memories of a vibrant and meaningful celebration!

## The Spring Talent Show

By Sam Liu (8Ti)

On March 4th, BASIS Independent Bellevue hosted one of the most exciting events of the year: the school talent show. Students, teachers, and families all gathered to watch, and the gym was full of that excitement. The show featured a wide mix of performances, each one showing a different side of the performers’ personalities they bring to school every day. Singing took up a big part of the show, with several students performing as well as a duet by Ms. Ankham and Mr. Jason. However, piano performances still took up a majority of the show with many beautiful pieces played by students throughout the grades. Some pieces were calm and peaceful, while others were dramatic and intense. One of the most exciting and unique acts of the show was a rhythm‑game performance by Lucas Fan of grade 10. It wasn’t something people expected to see on stage, which made it even more fun to watch. Good job to all of our performers!
