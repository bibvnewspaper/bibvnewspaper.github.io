## The Egg War

Written by Megna Prakash, 8Ti.

On March 6th, 8th grade students participated in the egg wars. The egg war is a bracket-style tournament, where students design a vehicle with the objective to protect their egg from a head-on collision. The Egg War gave 8th graders an opportunity to apply their knowledge of Physics from past years in a hands-on way. Constructing the Egg War vehicle required the students to apply many physics principles they learned in class.

The students used the concepts of Momentum conservation and impulse to analyze the conditions of the collision while building their vehicles, Newton’s 2nd Law to decrease the acceleration by increasing the mass, the Work- Energy Theorem to increase the stopping distance to increase the time of the collision for less acceleration, and the Law of Inertia to prove the benefits of increasing the mass of their vehicle. The competition was action-packed, and the results were beyond expectations. 
After the individual class tournaments, the winners from each 8th grade class faced off against each other. This year, the winner from 8Ti was Jasper Huang, and the winner from 8Pt was Emma Xi. The final competition between the winners from both cohorts was very fierce, with very close results, but Jasper took the victory. Jasper mentioned that he had not fully expected to win the Egg Wars.

“I knew I wouldn’t get out in the first round, but I also didn’t know that I would be in first place”, he mentioned.

Besides winning, there were many opportunities to receive awards and recognition, including the “Most Innovative” Award, “Best Engineered” Award, and the “Best Decorated” Award. Students who won second place in each cohort also received an award and recognition.
Jasper, the winner of the Egg Wars, mentioned that securing the egg was more important than trying to layer the egg with protection, because protecting the egg wouldn’t matter if the Egg fell in the first round. Jasper’s advice to the students participating in the Egg War next year was, “Try your best, and do a lot of research. Use soft materials to help protect the egg”. Jasper’s initiative to build something new showcased how important it was to be creative with the construction.
